<lzh>營寺安居利充食學處第二十八</lzh>
The 28th training precept: Supplementing one’s food with offerings intended for the running of the monastery during the rains retreat.

<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>緣處同前。時吐羅難陀作念：「所住居寺今皆破壞，誰當修補？」遂有婆羅門長者妻等，來禮尼足退坐一邊，尼說法要，復將諸女遍示破處。彼諸女人為欲修補，各減貲財淨心奉施。尼既得已，同前食噉。後時復來，見寺破落，問言：「聖者！何故猶不修理？」尼即報曰：「小房庵室足得安居，何用嚴飾？」聞已譏嫌：「云何苾芻尼得僧祇利物，迴入自己而為食用？」諸尼聞已白諸苾芻，苾芻白佛，佛以此緣，同前集尼問實訶責，廣說乃至「制其學處，應如是說：</lzh>
Location as before. Then Thullanandā thought: “The monastery where we’re staying is now totally dilapidated. Who’ll repair it?” Then female brahmin householders came, paid respect at the bhikkhunī’s feet, and sat at one side. The bhikkhunī spoke on the essentials of the dhamma, and also took the women everywhere to show the damaged places. The women wanted to repair them, and each donated a part of the funds with a pure heart. When the bhikkhunī had obtained them, she (bought) food and edibles as before. At a later time, they came again, saw that the monastery was damaged and caving in, and asked: “Noble one, why haven’t you repaired it yet?” The bhikkhunī then replied: “Small rooms and huts are sufficient for the rains retreat. What’s the use of adorning them?” When they heard this, they were upset: “How can a bhikkhunī obtain offerings and goods for the sangha, direct them to herself, and use them for food?” When the bhikkhunīs heard this, they told the bhikkhus, and the bhikkhus told the Buddha. The Buddha gathered the bhikkhunīs for this reason as before, asked if it was true, and criticized her, as explained in detail, down to: “I’ll lay down another training precept. <h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若復苾芻尼，得營寺利充食用者，泥薩祇波逸底迦。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī obtains offerings intended for the running of the monastery and uses them to supplement her food, it’s a nissaggiya pācittiya.’”<note>In the pātimokkha, the rule says: If a bhikkhunī obtains offerings for the rains retreat and uses them to supplement her food, it’s a nissaggiya pācittiya. The rule here in the vibhaṅga doesn’t mention the rains retreat. But curiously the title does.</note>

<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>尼等如上。</lzh>
“A bhikkhunī” is as above.

<lzh>營寺利者，謂施主本心與如來聲聞眾利，具如前說，</lzh>
“Offerings intended for the running of the monastery” means the donor’s original intention is to give offerings to the sangha of the disciples of the Tathāgata, as fully explained before.

<lzh>餘義亦同。</lzh>
The rest of the explanations is also the same.
