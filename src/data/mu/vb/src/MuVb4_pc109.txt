<lzh>年滿十二不與正學法授近圓學處第一百九</lzh>
The 109th training precept: Giving the full ordination to someone who is 12 years old, but hasn’t undertaken the sikkhamānā rules.

<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>緣處同前。時吐羅難陀苾芻尼，知曾嫁女人年滿十二得與出家，即自念言：「世尊聽許令受近圓。」不與正學法便授近圓。尼白苾芻，苾芻白佛。佛問訶責，廣說乃至「制其學處，應如是說：</lzh>
Location as before. At that time, Thullanandā bhikkhunī knew that married women who are 12 years old may be given the going forth. Then she thought: “The World-honored One has allowed to give the full ordination.” Without giving the sikkhamānā rules, she directly gave the full ordination. The bhikkhunīs told the bhikkhus, and the bhikkhus told the Buddha. The Buddha asked and criticized, as explained in detail, down to: “I’ll lay down another training precept. You should recite it like this: 

<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若復苾芻尼，知曾嫁女人年滿十二，不與正學法而受近圓者，波逸底迦。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī knows that a married woman is 12 years old, and gives her the full ordination without giving her the sikkhamānā rules, it’s a pācittiya.’”
