<lzh>教俗人呪法學處第一百五十一</lzh>
The 151st training precept: Teaching magic arts to a lay person.

<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>緣處同前。時有俗人來，從吐羅難陀尼求學呪法，尼即與之，呪曰：「呬里呬里普莎訶。」俗人聞已即便受得，尼復更授。彼便報曰：「聖者！我已受得，無勞更授。」尼雖聞告仍授不休，俗人忿怒報言：「我不須呪。」時有餘尼問言：「聖者！何意頻頻授人呪法？」答曰：「我愛此人，欲得共語為此頻授。」尼白苾芻，苾芻白佛。佛問吐羅難陀：「汝實如此頻授人法？」答言：「實爾。」廣說乃至「制其學處，應如是說：</lzh>
Location as before. Then a lay person came and asked to learn magic arts from Thullanandā bhikkhunī. The bhikkhunī then taught him a spell that said: “Xili xili pu suohe.” Having heard it, the lay person learned it, but the bhikkhunī still taught them again. They replied: “Noble one, I’ve already learned it. Don’t bother teaching it again.” Even though the bhikkhunī heard what they said, she still didn’t stop teaching them. The lay person replied angrily: “I don’t need the spell.” Then other bhikkhunīs asked: “Noble one, why do you teach magic arts to people again and again?” She answered: “I love this person and want to talk with them. Therefore I teach repeatedly.” The bhikkhunīs told the bhikkhus, and the bhikkhus told the Buddha. The Buddha asked Thullanandā: “Did you really repeatedly teach (magic) arts to people in this way?” She answered: “I really did.” As explained in detail, down to: “I’ll lay down another training precept. You should recite it like this: 

<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若復苾芻尼，教俗人呪法者，波逸底迦。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī teaches magic arts to a lay person, it’s a pācittiya.’”

<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>尼謂窣吐羅難陀等。</lzh>
“A bhikkhunī” means Thullanandā etc.

<lzh>教俗人呪法者，謂授他呪。</lzh>
“Teaching magic arts to a lay person” means teaching them spells.

<lzh>釋罪相等，廣說如前。</lzh>
The explanations of the rule factors etc. are as explained in detail before.
