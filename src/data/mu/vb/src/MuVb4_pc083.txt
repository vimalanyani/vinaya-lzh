<lzh>獨與男子露處立學處第八十三</lzh>
The 83rd training precept: Standing alone with a man outdoors.

<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>緣處同前。時吐羅難陀苾芻尼，獨與男子在露處立。婆羅門長者等見已生嫌，共相議曰：「此尼非是寂靜出家，遂與男子獨在露處共為期會。」尼白苾芻，苾芻白佛。佛問訶責廣如上說，乃至世尊「制其學處，應如是說：</lzh>
Location as before. Then Thullanandā bhikkhunī stood alone with a man outdoors. When the brahmin householders etc. had seen them, they got upset and said to each other: “This bhikkhunī hasn’t gone forth for extinguishment. She even has a date alone with a man outdoors.” The bhikkhunīs told the bhikkhus, and the bhikkhus told the Buddha. The Buddha asked and criticized, as explained in detail above, down to: “I’ll lay down another training precept. You should recite it like this: 

<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若復苾芻尼，獨與男子在露處立者，波逸底迦。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī stands alone with a man outdoors, it’s a pācittiya.’”

<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>若復苾芻尼者，謂吐羅尼，或復餘尼。</lzh>
“A bhikkhunī” means Thullā bhikkhunī, or any other bhikkhunī. 

<lzh>獨與男子者，謂白衣丈夫。</lzh>
“Alone with a man” means with a lay man.

<lzh>在露處立者，謂顯露無障。</lzh>
“Standing outdoors” means openly visible without obstructions. 

<lzh>乃至若有立者，皆得墮罪。</lzh>
… If they stand, they all incur a pācittiya offense.
