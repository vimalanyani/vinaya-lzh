<lzh>弟子有病不瞻視學處第九十九</lzh>
The 99th training precept: Not looking after a sick student.

<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>緣處同前。時吐羅難陀尼病，有親弟子及依止弟子皆為供侍。病得差已，後於異時，弟子等患無看病者，不淨狼藉不與除棄。諸苾芻尼互相問曰：「病者是誰？」答言：「吐羅尼弟子。」尼白苾芻，苾芻白佛。佛問訶責，廣說如前乃至「制其學處，應如是說：</lzh>
Location as before. Then Thullanandā bhikkhunī was sick. The students close to her and the students depending on her all waited upon her. When the illness had healed, at a later time, the students were sick and had nobody to nurse them. Their excrements made a mess, and nobody threw them out. The bhikkhunīs asked each other: “Who are these sick people?” Someone answered: “Thullā bhikkhunī’s students.” The bhikkhunīs told the bhikkhus, and the bhikkhus told the Buddha. The Buddha asked and criticized, as explained in detail, down to: “I’ll lay down another training precept. You should recite it like this: 

<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若復苾芻尼，於親弟子及依止弟子，見有病患不瞻侍者，波逸底迦。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī sees that the students close to her and the students depending on her are sick, and doesn’t take care of them, it’s a pācittiya.’”

<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>尼等如上。</lzh>
“A bhikkhunī” is as above. 

<lzh>親弟子者，謂與授近圓。</lzh>
“Students close to her” means to whom she has given the full ordination. 

<lzh>依止弟子者，謂依止而住。</lzh>
“Students depending on her” means who live in dependence on her. 

<lzh>病者，謂四大不調。</lzh>
“Sick” means that the four elements are out of balance. 

<lzh>不瞻侍者，謂不以慈心供給看養。</lzh>
“Not taking care” means not supporting and looking after them with a compassionate mind. 

<lzh>釋罪相等，廣說如前。</lzh>
The explanations of the rule factors etc. are as explained in detail before.
