<lzh>知有虎狼師子遊行學處第一百四</lzh>
The 104th training precept: Knowingly wandering where there are tigers, wolves, and lions.

<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>緣處同前。有諸尼眾於僻路遊行，多遭虎狼師子之厄，俗旅譏嫌，廣說乃至「制其學處，應如是說：</lzh>
Location as before. There were bhikkhunīs who were wandering on remote paths. They encountered much danger by tigers, wolves, and lions. The lay people were upset, as explained in detail, down to: “I’ll lay down another training precept. You should recite it like this: 

<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若復苾芻尼，知彼處有虎狼師子怖而遊行者，波逸底迦。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī knows that in a place there are dangers from tigers, wolves, or lions, and wanders there, it’s a pācittiya.’”

<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>尼等如上。</lzh>
“A bhikkhunī” is as above. 

<lzh>知彼有者，謂知有虎狼。</lzh>
“Knowing that in a place there are…” means knowing that there are tigers and wolves. 

<lzh>餘義可知。釋罪相等，亦如上說。</lzh>
The rest is clear. The explanations of the rule factors etc. are as explained above.
