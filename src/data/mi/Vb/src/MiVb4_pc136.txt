<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼擲糞掃及殘食於籬牆外，污泥人及非人。諸白衣見，譏訶言：「云何比丘尼隔牆擲糞掃，污泥於人？此等無有法則！」諸長老比丘尼聞，種種訶責，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs threw garbage and leftover food over fences and walls. They soiled and dirtied humans and non-humans. When the lay people saw it, they criticized them: “Why do bhikkhunīs throw garbage over the separation wall and soil and dirty people? They have no regulations.” When the senior bhikkhunīs heard it, they criticized them in all kinds of ways, … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，擲糞掃及殘食於籬牆外，若使人擲，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī throws garbage and leftover food over a fence or wall, or has others throw them, it’s a pācittiya.’”


<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>式叉摩那、沙彌尼，突吉羅。</lzh>
For a sikkhamānā and a sāmaṇerī, it’s a dukkaṭa.
