<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼往至邊地，邊地人抄取作婢，或奪衣鉢，或破梵行。諸長老比丘尼聞，種種呵責，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs went to border lands. The people of the border lands enslaved them, stole their robes and bowls, or raped them. When the senior bhikkhunīs heard it, they criticized them in all kinds of ways, … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，往邊地，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī goes to a border land, it’s a pācittiya.’”


<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>邊地者：無比丘、比丘尼處。</lzh>
“A border land” means a place without bhikkhus and bhikkhunīs.

<lzh>式叉摩那、沙彌尼，突吉羅。</lzh>
For a sikkhamānā and a sāmaṇerī, it’s a dukkaṭa.

<lzh>若飛行，不犯。</lzh>
If she flies there, the offense isn’t committed.
