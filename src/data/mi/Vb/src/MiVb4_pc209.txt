<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼種種自卜，亦從他卜。諸白衣譏呵言：「此等不捨邪見，何應得道？」諸長老比丘尼聞，種種呵責，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs engaged in all kinds of fortune telling, and they also asked others to tell their fortune. The lay people criticized them: “These people don’t give up wrong view. How could they attain the path?” When the senior bhikkhunīs heard it, they criticized them in all kinds of ways, … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，自卜，若就他卜，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī engages in fortune telling herself, or ask others to tell her fortune, it’s a pācittiya.’”

For a sikkhamānā and a sāmaṇerī, it’s a dukkaṭa.
