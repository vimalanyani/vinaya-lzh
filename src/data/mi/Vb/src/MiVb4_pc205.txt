<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼如妓女法著衣，生不樂道心，遂至反俗，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs wore their robes in the style of dancing girls. Unhappiness about the path arose in their minds. Thereupon, they returned to lay life. … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，如妓女法著衣，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī wears her robe in the style of dancing girls, it’s a pācittiya.’”


<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>式叉摩那、沙彌尼，突吉羅。</lzh>
For a sikkhamānā and a sāmaṇerī, it’s a dukkaṭa.
