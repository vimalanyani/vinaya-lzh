<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時差摩比丘尼無住止處，詣一居士語言：「我無住止處，為我作精舍！」居士答如前，便與物。得物已，作是念：「我衣麁弊壞，當用作之，住處自多。」便用作衣。作衣竟，往語居士，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, Khemā bhikkhunī didn’t have a place to stay. She went to a householder and said: “I don’t have a place to stay. Build a monastery for me.” The householder answered as before, and gave items. Having obtained the items, she thought: “My robes are rough, worn out, and breaking up. I’ll use (these items) to make some. There are plenty of places to stay.” Then she used them to make robes. Having made robes, she went and told the householder … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus:


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，自為是事，從一居士乞；自作餘用者，尼薩耆波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī asks for something for herself from a householder, and then uses it herself for another purpose, it’s a nissaggiya pācittiya.’”
