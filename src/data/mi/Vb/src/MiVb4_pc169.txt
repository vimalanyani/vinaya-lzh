<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼夜輒開都門出，不語後人令閉，夜有賊來，奪諸比丘尼衣鉢。諸長老比丘尼種種呵責，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs just opened the gate of the monastic compound at night and went out. They didn’t tell the people who stayed behind to have it closed. At night, criminals came and stole the bhikkhunīs’ robes and bowls. The senior bhikkhunīs criticized them in all kinds of ways, … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，夜輒開都門出，不語餘比丘尼令閉，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī just opens the gate of the monastic compound at night, goes out, and doesn’t tell the other bhikkhunīs to have it closed, it’s a pācittiya.’”


<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>式叉摩那、沙彌尼，突吉羅。</lzh>
For a sikkhamānā and a sāmaṇerī, it’s a dukkaṭa.

<lzh>無恐怖處，不犯。</lzh>
If it’s not a frightening place, the offense isn’t committed.
