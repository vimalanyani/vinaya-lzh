<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼照鏡，生不樂道心，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs looked at themselves in a mirror. Unhappiness about the path arose in their minds. … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，照鏡，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī looks at herself in a mirror, it’s a pācittiya.’”


<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>若水中照，突吉羅。</lzh>
If she looks at herself in water, it’s a dukkaṭa.

<lzh>式叉摩那、沙彌尼，突吉羅。</lzh>
For a sikkhamānā and a sāmaṇerī, it’s a dukkaṭa.

<lzh>若面有瘡，照看，不犯。</lzh>
If she has an abscess on the face and she looks to see it, the offense isn’t committed.
