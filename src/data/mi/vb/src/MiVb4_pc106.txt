<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼，與未滿十八歲童女受學戒，愚闇無知不堪學戒。諸長老比丘尼見，種種訶責，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs gave girls who weren’t yet 18 years old the training precepts<note>i.e. sikkhamānā ordination.</note>. They were ignorant and without knowledge, and couldn’t bear to train in the precepts. When the senior bhikkhunīs saw it, they criticized them in all kinds of ways, … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，與未滿十八歲童女受學戒，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī gives a girl who isn’t yet 18 years old the training precepts, it’s a pācittiya.’”


<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>童女者：未經男子。</lzh>
“A girl” means not yet having experience with a man.

<lzh>發心及方便，乃至白二羯磨未滿，突吉羅。白二羯磨滿，波逸提；餘師眾，皆突吉羅。</lzh>
If she thinks about and plans it, up to when the formal act with a motion as second isn’t yet finished, it’s a dukkaṭa. If the formal act with a motion as second is finished, it’s a pācittiya (for the preceptor). For the other teachers and the sangha, it’s a dukkaṭa each.
