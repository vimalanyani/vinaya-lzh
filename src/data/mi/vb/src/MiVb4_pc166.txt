<h2>Origin Story</h2>

<lzh>爾時諸比丘尼自煮生物作食，諸白衣譏呵言：「云何比丘尼自煮生物？既自煮作食，何為復就人乞耶？無沙門行，破沙門法！」諸長老比丘尼聞，種種呵責，乃至今為諸比丘尼結戒，亦如上說。「從今是戒應如是說：</lzh>
At that time, the bhikkhunīs themselves cooked raw things to prepare food. The lay people criticized them: “Why do the bhikkhunīs themselves cook raw things? When they’ve already cooked and prepared food, why do they then also ask for alms from people? This isn’t the practice of renunciates. They’ve broken the rules of renunciates.” When the senior bhikkhunīs heard it, they criticized them in all kinds of ways, … “I now lay down a precept for the bhikkhunīs, as explained above. From now on, this precept should be recited thus: 


<h2>Final Ruling</h2>

<lzh>「若比丘尼，自煮生物作食，波逸提。」</lzh>
‘If a bhikkhunī herself cooks raw things to prepare food, it’s a pācittiya.’”


<h2>Explanations</h2>

<lzh>若為病，不犯。</lzh>
If it’s for a sickness, the offense isn’t committed.
